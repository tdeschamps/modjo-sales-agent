# Coaching Themes — Sales Best-Practice Taxonomy

A **fixed, human-readable** set of coaching themes grounded in recognised sales methodology (MEDDPICC, SPIN, Winning by Design, Command of the Message, and conversation-intelligence benchmarks). These are the only themes the coaching skills may use. Use the **exact display name** — never a kebab-case slug, never an invented label.

**Two hard rules:**
1. Every observation maps to one of the themes below. If nothing fits, the skill does NOT invent a new theme inside a report — it surfaces `Candidate new theme: "<name>"` and a human decides whether to add it here.
2. The display name shown to users is the full sentence-case name (e.g. "Multi-threading", not `multi-thread`). Trackers and reports show the readable name only.

---

## Discovery & qualification

**Discovery depth** — Getting past surface needs to the real business pain, the trigger event, and who feels it. *Good looks like*: the rep can state the prospect's pain in the prospect's own words, plus the cost of leaving it unsolved.

**Qualification rigour** — Honest MEDDPICC scoring; not inflating a deal to protect the forecast. *Good looks like*: the rep can name the gap on every pillar and what would close it.

**Quantifying business impact** — Putting real numbers to the problem and the value. *Good looks like*: a metric the buyer agreed to, tied to a baseline (e.g. "12 reps × 4h/week of manual CRM work").

## Conversation execution

**Talk-to-listen ratio** — Letting the buyer do most of the talking in discovery. *Good looks like*: rep talks <45% in discovery, <55% in demo.

**Questioning technique** — Open, layered questions that dig past the first answer. *Good looks like*: follow-up questions that go a level deeper ("why does that matter to you?").

**Active listening** — Catching and following the buyer's signals instead of waiting to talk. *Good looks like*: the rep paraphrases and builds on what the buyer said.

**Objection handling** — Acknowledging, reframing, and advancing — not deflecting or capitulating. *Good looks like*: names the objection, reframes on value, gets a micro-commitment.

## Deal & account strategy

**Multi-threading** — Engaging multiple stakeholders, not riding one contact. *Good looks like*: 3+ engaged contacts with mapped roles on any material deal.

**Champion building** — Developing an internal advocate and testing whether they'll actually fight for you. *Good looks like*: the rep has asked "what would block this internally?" and armed the champion with differentiators.

**Mutual action plan** — Co-owning a written path from today to signature. *Good looks like*: a shared plan with dated steps and named owners on both sides.

**Competitive positioning** — Knowing who else is in the deal and differentiating cleanly. *Good looks like*: rep knows the competitor, the buyer's lean, and our two clearest differentiators.

## Commercial

**Value-based pricing** — Holding price and anchoring on value rather than pre-emptive discounting. *Good looks like*: rep ties price to the quantified impact, doesn't flinch first.

**Negotiation discipline** — Trading concessions, never giving them away. *Good looks like*: every concession is exchanged for something (timeline, multi-year, references).

## Rigour & execution

**Next-step discipline** — Every meeting ends with a booked next step and an owner. *Good looks like*: a calendar invite confirmed before the call ends.

**Pipeline generation** — Consistent net-new prospecting so the funnel doesn't run dry. *Good looks like*: a steady weekly cadence of new qualified opportunities, not just working old inventory.

**Forecast & CRM hygiene** — Accurate stages, close dates, and deal data. *Good looks like*: no deals open past close date; close dates reflect real conviction.

**Time & territory focus** — Spending time on the deals and accounts that matter. *Good looks like*: the highest-value deals get the most attention; dead deals get disqualified fast.

## Cross-functional (use sparingly, only when clearly the issue)

**Escalation & teaming** — Pulling in managers, specialists, or leadership at the right moment instead of absorbing problems solo. *Good looks like*: the rep escalates a blocker or a teammate-caused issue fast, with a clear ask.

**Land-and-expand** — Turning a new win into an expansion path while the champion is hot. *Good looks like*: an expansion-scoping conversation booked within days of signature.

## How themes render in reports and trackers

- Always the full readable name: "Champion building", "Pipeline generation".
- In the theme tracker, each theme gets a plain status word, not a cryptic arrow alone:
  - **Strength** — consistently strong, reinforce it
  - **Improving** — was a gap, now trending up with evidence
  - **Needs work** — flagged again, no change yet
  - **Watch** — early signal, not yet a pattern
  - **New this week** — first time observed
- Pair the word with one short evidence phrase ("multi-threaded the deal across 11 stakeholders"). Never a bare symbol.
