# BANT — qualification rubric variant

Use this file's content to replace `shared/qualification-rubric.md` if your team uses BANT (Budget / Authority / Need / Timeline).

## Pillars

| Pillar | 0 — Missing | 1 — Partial | 2 — Validated |
|---|---|---|---|
| **B — Budget** | No budget discussion. | Budget mentioned ("we have some budget"), no number. | Specific number confirmed for this initiative, with a timeline of when it's available. |
| **A — Authority** | We don't know who signs. | We've heard a name, never spoken to them. | Direct contact with the decision maker; we know what they need to say yes. |
| **N — Need** | Pain unclear or surface-level. | Pain named, no urgency. | Specific pain + cost of inaction + the prospect's own words about why now. |
| **T — Timeline** | Unknown. | "Soon" or "this year" — no specific event or deadline. | Specific event-driven deadline tied to a business outcome. |

**Total**: 0–8. Bands:

- **0–2** — Cold. Disqualify or run disco.
- **3–5** — Warm. Working but gaps to close.
- **6–8** — Hot. Move toward close plan.

## Output line format

```
RUBRIC: B:2 A:1 N:2 T:1 | Total: 6/8 | Methodology: BANT
```

## Notes

BANT is more lightweight than MEDDPICC — works for SMB-shape deals and short cycles. Less effective for enterprise deals where Decision Process / Paper Process / Champion / Competition matter more. If you're running BANT but losing late-stage enterprise deals to procurement, consider upgrading to MEDDIC or MEDDPICC.
