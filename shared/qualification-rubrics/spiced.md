# SPICED — qualification rubric variant

Use this file's content to replace `shared/qualification-rubric.md` if your team uses SPICED (Situation / Pain / Impact / Critical Event / Decision) — Winning by Design's framework.

## Pillars

| Pillar | 0 — Missing | 1 — Partial | 2 — Validated |
|---|---|---|---|
| **S — Situation** | No clear picture of current state. | Situation roughly understood, gaps. | Clear picture: org, tools, workflows, who does what. |
| **P — Pain** | No pain articulated. | Surface pain, no impact named. | Quantified pain + emotional weight in the buyer's words. |
| **I — Impact** | Cost of inaction unclear. | Cost mentioned, not quantified. | Specific cost ($, time, lost deals, churn) tied to the pain. |
| **C — Critical event** | No deadline or trigger. | "Sometime this year" — soft. | Specific dated event that creates urgency (deadline, regulation, leadership change). |
| **D — Decision** | Decision process unknown. | Some steps known, no map. | Mapped: who decides, what criteria, what process, when. |

**Total**: 0–10. Bands:

- **0–3** — Early discovery.
- **4–7** — Active selling.
- **8–10** — Closeable.

## Output line format

```
RUBRIC: S:2 P:2 I:1 C:1 D:1 | Total: 7/10 | Methodology: SPICED
```

## Notes

SPICED is conversation-first and pain-centric — good fit for consultative sales and product-led companies. The "Critical Event" pillar is what makes it stronger than BANT for urgency. Less rigorous than MEDDPICC on Champion / Competition / Paper Process — if you lose deals to internal politics or procurement, those gaps will hurt.
